01 / About

Academic formation, research direction, and the person behind the work.

I am a second-year PhD scholar in Computer Science and Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Ropar. My research lies in high-performance computing and distributed systems, with a present focus on reducing communication overhead across large-scale environments.

I am especially interested in the behavior of MPI collective communication, synchronization, communication-aware performance modeling, trace-driven simulation, scalable parallel algorithms, and distributed AI workloads.

My long-term aim is to build a rigorous academic career in computing systems: producing research that is technically exact, useful in practice, and taught with clarity to future engineers and researchers.
