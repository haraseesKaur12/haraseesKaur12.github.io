A research program centered on the cost of coordination.

Central question

When a computation is distributed across many machines, how can we reduce the work spent communicating so that more of the system is devoted to useful progress?

Collective communication

Understanding MPI collectives as first-order performance objects rather than background operations, with attention to synchronization, data movement, and scalability.

Performance modeling

Building communication-aware models and trace-driven simulations that reveal where distributed execution loses time before those losses harden into design decisions.

Distributed coordination

Studying how large systems organize work, how communication patterns form, and how topology-aware decisions can reduce avoidable overhead.

Future workloads

Extending communication-centric reasoning toward heterogeneous systems, distributed AI, data-parallel training, and the next generation of scalable computing platforms.
