About me

Young researcher. Long horizon.

I am a PhD scholar at the Indian Institute of Technology Ropar, drawn to the hidden cost of coordination inside modern computing systems. My work focuses on reducing communication overhead across distributed environments so that large systems spend less time waiting and more time doing meaningful work.

I am especially interested in MPI collective communication, synchronization, communication performance analysis, trace-driven modeling, distributed AI, and scalable parallel algorithms. I want my research to sit where rigor meets usefulness: elegant enough for theory, concrete enough to improve real systems.

Research map

Reducing the drag between machines.

Communication-centric HPC

Designing systems around the cost of data movement, synchronization, and collective communication rather than treating them as afterthoughts.

Distributed coordination

Studying how nodes exchange information, where bottlenecks emerge, and how to orchestrate work with less waiting across large systems.

Performance modeling

Using trace-driven simulation and scalability-aware analysis to understand system behavior before costly inefficiencies become architecture.

Future systems

Extending these ideas toward heterogeneous platforms, distributed AI, and teaching the next generation to reason about systems at scale.
