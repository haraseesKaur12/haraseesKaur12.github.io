Research position

I study the communication costs that appear when many machines must coordinate as one system. My current direction is to make those costs visible, measurable, and easier to reason about before they become scalability bottlenecks in large distributed workloads.

Current work

Communication-aware systems research

My work connects low-level communication behavior with the decisions researchers and engineers make when designing large-scale systems.

  • Analyze synchronization and communication overheads in collective operations.
  • Build models that connect traces, topology, and system-level performance outcomes.
  • Explore how communication-aware reasoning transfers to distributed AI workloads.
